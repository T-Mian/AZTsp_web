The MIG welding method is based on the use of an inert gas, such as argon, which protects the welding area from reacting with the surroundings. It is a fast and effective metal joining technique, especially when welding non-ferrous metals such as aluminum or copper. Thanks to precise regulation of current and gas, we are able to ensure accurate, strong and durable welds.




MIG (Metal Inert Gas) welding:

MIG

The MIG method, also known as inert gas welding, involves powering a conductive welding wire melted in a welding torch with electrical current. At the same time, an inert gas is supplied to the welding area, most often argon or a mixture of argon with carbon dioxide or nitrogen oxide, which protects the molten metal drops from oxidation in the atmosphere.


Advantages:

The MIG method is fast and effective, which makes it ideal for welding larger surfaces or in series production.
It is suitable for welding a variety of metals and their alloys, including carbon steel, stainless steel, aluminum and copper.
The welding process is relatively simple and easy to learn, allowing you to achieve results quickly.



Usage:

☐Production of motor vehicles, steel structures, metal processing and industrial structures.
☐ Welding in production plants, where speed and efficiency are key.






MAG welding:

MAG

The MAG method, also known as active gas welding, is similar to the MIG method, with the difference that instead of pure inert gas, a mixture of inert gases with the addition of active gases, such as carbon dioxide or nitrogen oxide, is used.


Advantages:

High quality welds: The MAG method allows you to obtain perfect welds even at higher welding speeds.
Resistance to pollution: The addition of active gases increases the resistance of the weld to atmospheric pollution.
Application in various conditions: This method is suitable for welding both in industrial and outdoor conditions.


Usage:

Steel structures, shipbuilding industry, energy industry, chemical industry.
Welding of steel with medium and large thicknesses, where strength and durability of the weld are required.

TiG (Tungsten Inert Gas) is a method that uses electrically heated tungsten electrodes and an inert gas such as argon. This is a popular technique in welding stainless steel, aluminum and other metals with low thermal conductivity. It is characterized by aesthetic, clean and precise welds, ideal for applications requiring a high-quality finish.




TIG welding:

TIG

The TIG method, also known as tungsten inert gas welding, is one of the most precise welding techniques. It involves using an electrically heated tungsten rod as an electrode that is not melted during the welding process. An inert gas, usually pure argon, is supplied to the welding area.


Advantages:

Precision: The TIG method enables precise welding of even the most delicate and complex details.
Cleanliness: The welding process takes place without the use of additional filler material, which ensures clean and aesthetic welds.
Heat control: Thanks to precise regulation of current and gas, it is possible to precisely control the welding temperature.


Usage:

Welding of stainless steel, aluminum, copper and other metals with high-quality finish.
Applications where aesthetics and precision are key, such as the automotive industry, aerospace, and the production of medical devices and laboratory equipment.
